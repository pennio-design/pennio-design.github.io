The Pattern

The cycle killing African e-commerce brands.

The pattern is consistent enough to be predictable.

Phase 01
Get a Generic Logo
Something is designed before anything is understood.
Phase 02
Run Ads
The budget goes to Meta before the brand has earned the right to be amplified.
Phase 03
Ads Fail
Customer acquisition cost (CAC) climbs because there is nothing keeping people once they arrive.
Phase 04
Vanish
Without retention or reputation, the economics eventually break. The brand folds quietly.
Ads amplify what exists.
If nothing meaningful exists,
ads accelerate the burn.
The brands building real equity in Africa right now did not get there by finding a better-performing ad set. They got there because something about them was worth believing in before the first purchase. That does not happen by accident, and it does not come from a logo.
The Solution

The E-Commerce Foundation

01
Meaning Audit
